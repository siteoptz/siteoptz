## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy defines how [COMPANY NAME] employees and contractors may use artificial intelligence (AI) tools — including generative AI assistants, AI-powered coding tools, and any third-party service that uses machine learning to process our data — in the course of their work.

The goals of this policy are to:

- Let the team move fast with AI without creating legal, security, or reputational risk.
- Protect our customers' data, our intellectual property, and confidential information.
- Keep us ready to answer AI risk questions from customers, investors, and auditors.

This policy applies to **all use of AI tools for company work**, whether the tool is paid for by the company, free, or personal. It applies on company devices and personal devices alike when used for company business.

## 2. Definitions

- **AI tool** — any software that uses machine learning or generative AI to produce text, code, images, audio, video, analysis, or decisions. Examples: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, GitHub Copilot, Midjourney, and AI features embedded in tools you already use.
- **Customer data** — any data provided by or about our customers or their end users.
- **Confidential information** — non-public company information, including source code, financials, roadmaps, contracts, and employee or candidate data.
- **Consumer AI tool** — a free or personal-tier AI service whose terms allow the provider to train on your inputs (e.g., the free tier of many chat assistants).
- **Enterprise AI tool** — a paid/business-tier AI service with a contract, a Data Processing Agreement (DPA), and terms that prohibit training on your data.
- **Shadow AI** — any AI tool used for work that has not been reviewed and approved under this policy.

## 4. Acceptable Use

You **may** use approved AI tools to:

- Draft, edit, and summarize internal documents, emails, and content.
- Brainstorm, research publicly available information, and explore ideas.
- Write, review, and debug code, subject to the data rules in Section 6.
- Automate repetitive tasks that do not involve customer, employee, or regulated data.

Always apply human judgment. **You are responsible for any output you use** — including its accuracy, originality, and appropriateness. Review AI output before relying on it, sharing it, or shipping it to customers.

## 5. Prohibited Uses

You **may not**:

- Enter **customer data**, **confidential information**, **personal data**, or **regulated data** (e.g., health, financial, or biometric data) into a **consumer AI tool** or any unapproved tool.
- Paste **proprietary source code** into consumer AI tools.
- Use AI to make a final decision that materially affects a person — hiring, firing, credit, pricing, or access — **without human review** of that decision.
- Present AI-generated work as independently verified when it has not been (e.g., legal, medical, or financial claims).
- Use AI to generate content that is discriminatory, harassing, deceptive, infringing, or otherwise unlawful.
- Bypass, disable, or misconfigure the security or privacy settings of an approved AI tool.

## 6. Data Handling Rules

1. **Classify before you paste.** Before entering anything into an AI tool, ask: is this public, internal, customer, or regulated data?
2. **Public and internal data** may be used with approved tools.
3. **Customer, personal, and regulated data** may only be used with an **enterprise AI tool** that is on the approved list, has a DPA in place, and does not train on our data — and only where there is a legitimate business need.
4. **Default to enterprise tiers.** Where the company provides a business/enterprise account for a tool, use it. Do not use the personal or free tier for work.
5. **Minimize.** Share only the data the task actually requires. Redact identifiers where possible.
6. **No training without approval.** Do not opt our data into model training, and do not enable data-sharing features without sign-off from the policy owner.

## 7. Intellectual Property & Confidentiality

- AI output is **not automatically protected by copyright** and may resemble others' work. Do not assume AI-generated content is original or that we own it. Treat it as a draft to be reviewed and substantially edited.
- Do not input third-party confidential information (e.g., a partner's or customer's secrets) into AI tools.
- Do not use AI in a way that violates a customer or vendor contract, an NDA, or a software license.
- Anything you produce with AI for the company remains the company's confidential information and is subject to the same handling rules as any other work product.

## 8. Vendor & New Tool Approval Process

Before using a **new AI tool** for anything beyond public information:

1. Submit a request to the policy owner (or [DESIGNATED CHANNEL — e.g., #ai-tools Slack]).
2. The owner runs the **AI Vendor Review Checklist** (companion template) covering security certifications, data handling, training terms, and a DPA.
3. If approved, the tool is added to the approved list and the AI Tool Inventory.
4. No tool touches customer or regulated data until this review is complete.

Target turnaround for a review: **[e.g., 3 business days]**.

## 9. Human Oversight & Accountability

- A human must review AI output before it is sent to a customer, published externally, or used to make a decision about a person.
- The employee who uses AI output **owns the outcome**, including errors. "The AI said so" is not a defense.
- High-impact use cases (anything affecting customers, money, safety, or legal standing) require a second reviewer.

## 10. Training & Awareness

- Every new hire reviews and acknowledges this policy during onboarding.
- The company runs an AI use refresher at least **[annually]**, and whenever this policy materially changes.
- Questions about whether a use is allowed should go to the policy owner **before** acting, not after.

## 11. Reporting Incidents

If you suspect that customer data, confidential information, or personal data has been exposed to an unapproved tool — or that an AI output has caused harm — report it **immediately** to:

**[NAME / ROLE]** — **[EMAIL]** — **[PHONE / SLACK]**

Report promptly even if you are unsure. Early reporting limits damage and is never penalized. Incidents are handled under our **AI Incident Response Plan**.

## 12. Monitoring & Enforcement

- The company may monitor the use of company-provided AI tools and accounts to enforce this policy and meet security obligations.
- Violations may result in revoked access, disciplinary action up to and including termination, and, where applicable, legal action.
- Enforcement is proportionate: honest mistakes that are reported promptly are treated very differently from deliberate or concealed violations.

## 13. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed at least **[annually]** and whenever there is a significant change in our AI tools, our regulatory obligations, or applicable law. The policy owner is responsible for keeping it current.
