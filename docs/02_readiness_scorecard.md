## THE 10 QUESTIONS

Each question scored 0, 3, 7, or 10. Total possible: 100.

---

### Q1. AI Tool Inventory

**Could you list every AI tool, model, and API your company uses today — including the ones individual employees signed up for?**

- **(0 pts)** No — we genuinely don't know what's in use
- **(3 pts)** Roughly — we know the main ones, but shadow AI is a black box
- **(7 pts)** Yes for sanctioned tools — we have a list, updated occasionally
- **(10 pts)** Yes, comprehensively — auto-tracked, updated continuously, includes shadow AI

---

### Q2. Data Sensitivity Mapping

**Do you know which AI tools touch customer data, employee data, or other sensitive information?**

- **(0 pts)** No — we haven't mapped this
- **(3 pts)** Some — we know our customer-facing AI features, but not internal use
- **(7 pts)** Mostly — we have a data flow diagram for sanctioned tools
- **(10 pts)** Yes — every tool is classified by data sensitivity and documented

---

### Q3. Vendor Due Diligence

**Before adopting a new AI vendor, do you review their SOC 2, data residency, sub-processors, and AI-specific terms?**

- **(0 pts)** No — we sign up and start using
- **(3 pts)** Sometimes — for big purchases, not for individual seats
- **(7 pts)** Yes for new procurement, but legacy tools were never reviewed
- **(10 pts)** Yes for everything, with a formal vendor review checklist on file

---

### Q4. AI Acceptable Use Policy

**Do you have a written AI acceptable use policy that employees have read and acknowledged?**

- **(0 pts)** No policy exists
- **(3 pts)** Draft policy exists but isn't communicated or enforced
- **(7 pts)** Policy exists, employees know about it, but no formal acknowledgment
- **(10 pts)** Policy exists, every employee has signed it, refreshed annually

---

### Q5. Regulatory Classification

**Have you classified your AI systems against the EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, or other regulations that apply to your business?**

- **(0 pts)** No — we haven't done this exercise
- **(3 pts)** We know roughly which regulations apply, but not which of our systems fall under each
- **(7 pts)** Partial mapping done — main systems classified, edge cases not
- **(10 pts)** Yes — every AI system is classified, documented, and reviewed quarterly

---

### Q6. Risk Register

**If an enterprise buyer asked for your AI risk register today, could you produce it within 48 hours?**

- **(0 pts)** No — we'd have to build it from scratch
- **(3 pts)** We could pull something together, but it wouldn't be comprehensive
- **(7 pts)** Yes, we have one — but it's not current or formatted for external use
- **(10 pts)** Yes — current, comprehensive, and ready to share

---

### Q7. Customer & Contractual Obligations

**Have you reviewed your customer contracts and DPAs to identify AI-related commitments (e.g., "we will not train on your data")?**

- **(0 pts)** No — we haven't audited contracts for AI clauses
- **(3 pts)** Aware in principle, but no systematic review
- **(7 pts)** Reviewed for top customers, not the long tail
- **(10 pts)** Complete contract review, with controls in place to honor each commitment

---

### Q8. Team Training

**Has your team received training on responsible AI use, data handling, and policy compliance in the last 12 months?**

- **(0 pts)** No formal training
- **(3 pts)** Informal — shared a Slack message or a link to an article
- **(7 pts)** Yes, but only for technical staff or only at onboarding
- **(10 pts)** Yes — company-wide, refreshed annually, with completion tracked

---

### Q9. Incident Response

**Do you have a documented plan for what happens if an AI tool leaks data, produces a discriminatory output, or fails in production?**

- **(0 pts)** No plan
- **(3 pts)** Generic incident response plan exists but no AI-specific procedures
- **(7 pts)** AI-specific plan drafted but never tested
- **(10 pts)** Plan exists, tested via tabletop exercises, with named owners

---

### Q10. Executive Ownership

**Is AI governance owned by a named executive with the authority to enforce policy and stop non-compliant deployments?**

- **(0 pts)** No — it's nobody's job
- **(3 pts)** Distributed across founders/leadership informally
- **(7 pts)** Named owner, but limited authority or bandwidth
- **(10 pts)** Yes — named executive, board-level visibility, regular reporting

## SCORING & RESULTS PAGE

### Score Bands

**0–30: Critical Risk 🔴**
Your AI stack is currently undocumented and unaccountable. Most enterprise procurement and Series A diligence would stop here. You need foundational work — an inventory, a policy, and an owner — in the next 30 days. **Priority: stop the bleeding.**

**31–60: Foundational Gaps 🟡**
You have the basics in place but significant gaps remain. You'd likely fail an enterprise AI questionnaire or a sophisticated VC's diligence. The good news: most of this is fixable in 60–90 days with the right templates and process. **Priority: build the audit packet.**

**61–85: Mostly Ready 🟢**
You have a real governance posture. You'd pass most diligence, but specific frameworks (EU AI Act, ISO 42001) and edge cases (shadow AI, vendor sub-processors) are likely where you'll get challenged. **Priority: harden the edges.**

**86–100: Audit-Ready 🏆**
You're in the top 5% of companies your size. You're ready for enterprise deals, sophisticated diligence, and regulatory audits. **Priority: maintain — quarterly reviews and continuous monitoring.**

### Personalized Output (PDF Template)

For each user, generate:

1. **Total score** (large, color-coded by band)
2. **Score breakdown by category** (radar chart across 10 dimensions)
3. **Your top 3 gaps** — the three lowest-scoring questions, each with:
   - The risk this gap creates (deal blocker / regulatory exposure / audit fail)
   - The 30-day fix (concrete first action)
   - The SiteOptz resource that solves it (link to relevant tier or asset)
4. **Benchmark** — "Companies your size scored an average of X. You scored Y."
5. **Recommended next step** based on band:
   - 0–30 → Book a free AI risk strategy call
   - 31–60 → Download the Founder's Guide + Inventory Template
   - 61–85 → Compare Pro vs. Enterprise tier
   - 86–100 → Talk about Enterprise certification path
